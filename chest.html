Bench Press


The bench press is a fundamental strength training exercise that involves lying on a bench and lifting a barbell or dumbbells above the chest. It primarily targets the chest, shoulders, and triceps muscles. While lying on the bench, you lower the weight to your chest and then press it back up, engaging multiple muscle groups.






Cable flies


Cable flies are a strength training exercise that targets the chest muscles. Using a cable machine, stand in the center, holding handles in each hand. Start with hands at shoulder height, elbows slightly bent. With controlled motion, bring hands together in front of your chest, engaging the chest muscles. Slowly return to the starting position, maintaining tension on the cables.






Dumbell Press


The dumbbell press is a weightlifting exercise targeting the chest, shoulders, and triceps. While lying on a bench, hold a dumbbell in each hand at chest level. Push the dumbbells upward while exhaling, extending your arms. Lower them back down to chest level with control. The exercise enhances upper body strength, muscle growth, and stability.
