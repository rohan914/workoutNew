Deadlift


The deadlift is a compound weightlifting movement that involves lifting a barbell or weight from the ground to a standing position. Begin with feet hip-width apart, bend at the hips and knees to grasp the bar with an overhand or mixed grip. Lift the bar while keeping a flat back, engaging the legs, back, and core muscles. Stand upright by extending hips and knees.






Barbell rows


Barbell rows are a compound strength exercise. Begin by bending at the hips, knees slightly bent, and hold a barbell with an overhand grip. With a straight back, pull the barbell towards your lower abdomen, squeezing your shoulder blades together. Lower the barbell back down with control. Barbell rows engage the upper back, lats, and biceps, enhancing strength and posture.






Lat pull down


Lat pulldown is a strength exercise targeting the upper back and latissimus dorsi muscles. Sit at the pulldown machine, grip the bar wider than shoulder-width, and pull it down to your chest while exhaling. Squeeze your shoulder blades together and engage your lats. Slowly release the bar to the starting position.
